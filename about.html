About

Two practitioners.
Sixty-five years in the making.

Swift Supply Solutions Inc. was built on a simple premise: the organizations that need the most experienced operational expertise are rarely the ones that can justify the overhead of a large consulting firm to get it. We solve that problem — directly, without layers, from practitioners who have done the work at scale.

The Practice

Senior expertise applied directly. No layers in between.

Swift Supply Solutions Inc. is a boutique consulting and fractional executive practice focused on print, mail, transactional document operations, and digital transformation. We work with financial services, insurance, healthcare, and regulated organizations — and with print service providers of all sizes navigating operational challenges, technology transitions, or the move to high-speed inkjet.

Our methodology is built around three fundamentals — People, Process, and Technology — applied in that order, every time. We bring an automation-first mindset to every engagement, designing no-touch workflows before recommending technology spend, and using the right tools — process redesign, AI-assisted analysis, operational dashboards and reporting — based on what the client's environment can actually support and sustain.

The goal is not a report. The goal is an operation that runs better, costs less, and gives leadership the visibility to manage it.

Where It Started

Same institution. Different tracks. Sixty-five years of combined experience.

Both Gabe and Mike Preczner began their careers at Moore Business Communications Services — a division of Moore Corporation Limited, which was at the time the largest printing and business forms company in the world, with operations in more than 40 countries and over 25,000 employees globally.

From that shared foundation, they took different paths through the same industry. Gabe moved into senior operational leadership and executive roles — managing P&L, leading transformations, and building the strategic and organizational capability that produces lasting change. Mike stayed closer to the production floor and systems layer — building and operating some of Canada's largest regulated print and mail environments at enterprise scale.

Together, those two tracks cover the full stack. From board-level transformation strategy to production floor workflow design. From vendor contract negotiation to ADF architecture and MICR certification. That combination is rare. And it is the foundation of what Swift Supply Solutions delivers.

What We Bring Together

Full-stack operational expertise.

The combination of a strategic executive track and a deep technical production track means we can engage at any level of the operation — and connect the two.

⚙

No-Touch Workflow Design

Automation-first thinking applied to every engagement. We map the manual steps, quantify the cost and error risk, and design workflows that eliminate them — from data composition through to mail stream.

📈

Operational Intelligence & Reporting

Building the visibility layer alongside the operational fix. Power BI dashboards, KPI frameworks, and operational reporting designed for the client's environment — so leadership can manage by exception, not by firefighting.

💻

Toner-to-Inkjet Transition

One of the most technically demanding transitions in transactional print — substrate development, colour profiling, ADF reconfiguration, white paper factory conversion. We have done it at enterprise scale and can guide or deliver it end to end.

📄

ADF & Document Integrity

Automated Document Factory architecture, 2D barcode piece-level tracking, closed-loop reprint generation, and MICR-certified cheque production. The integrity layer that regulated clients require and most operations don't have fully built.

📦

Postal & Delivery Optimization

Presort strategy, commingling, address quality, Canada Post volume scheduling, and sortation discount negotiation. At $800M in annual postage managed, we know where the savings are and how to capture them.

📊

Vendor Portfolio Management

Contract benchmarking, RFP management, vendor consolidation, and ongoing governance. Print and mail vendor contracts that haven't been formally reviewed in three or more years are almost certainly not reflecting current market pricing.
